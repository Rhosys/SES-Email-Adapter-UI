03 The rule engine

Rules you can read like code.

A

Auto OTP forwarding

Auth codes detected on inbound, pushed via Web Push to the browser extension, autofilled into the focused login field on the matching origin.

B

Draft auto-creation from templates

Sandboxed JS template functions resolve against the live signal — order numbers, sender names, anything on the arc — at send time.

C

More than spam — also “unwanted”

A signal that's technically legit but you never want again (vendor newsletters, ex-client cold reach-outs) gets discarded by the rule engine, not deferred to a spam score.

D

Quarantine and block by default

New senders are held in quarantine until you allow them. Or set the disposition to silent block. Or to a hard SMTP 5xx reject. Your inbox, your rules.

Every inbound signal runs through your rule graph before it lands. Conditions are plain JavaScript expressions, evaluated in a sandbox. Actions are composable — label, quarantine, forward, auto-reply from a template, or reject at the SMTP layer.

04 Forwarding loops

Every edge case handled.

Numaeel reads the label graph at validation time. If a forwarding target's label set overlaps the source address's label set, the rule is rejected.

No retry storms. No duplicate auto-replies bouncing off a vacation responder until everyone's mailbox is full.

  • → Label-set intersection check at rule save and on every forwarding-target verification.
  • → Auto-reply gated on Reply-To domain matching From domain — the weaponised reply-to spam vector closes.
  • → Outbound recipient checked against the alias's approved-sender set before send — a compromised account can't be turned into a spam cannon.
05 Open source · JMAP

Open source on GitHub, not a walled garden.

Numaeel is built in the open. The backend, the Vue UI, and the browser extension live on GitHub under a permissive licence — fork them, audit them, run them yourself if you'd rather. 06 Calendar & identity

Keep your calendar. Keep your logins.

Numaeel is a privacy front-end for the mail you receive — not a replacement for the tools you already trust. Google Calendar, Apple Calendar, Outlook 365 — whatever you use, it stays where it is. Same with your existing Google, GitHub, Apple, or Microsoft logins. They don't change.

What changes is who sees them. Hand out a Numaeel alias instead of your real calendar address. Invites land on Numaeel, get verified, and are forwarded to your real calendar over a clean ICS link. Your accept, decline, and free/busy responses route back through the same alias — so the organiser hears from [email protected], never from your private mailbox.

  • → Two-way ICS pass-through: invite, RSVP, update, cancel — all flow through the alias.
  • → Spoofed organisers, fake meeting bombs, and ICS trackers are dropped at the proxy.
  • → Burn a calendar alias the moment it starts leaking — your real calendar address never changes.
